# HabitOS v2 — The Coach Upgrade

**Release date:** 2026-04-08
**Version:** v2.0.0
**Audience:** existing HabitOS users + landing-page visitors

HabitOS v2 turns the app from a habit tracker into a real personal coach. You bring a goal and a few minutes; HabitOS brings the framework, the plan, the reminders, and the persona that fits how you actually want to be coached. Everything is grounded in the same evidence base we have always used: GROW, WOOP, Atomic Habits Identity, and the implementation-intentions research that makes habits stick.

This release ships in five layers, each useful on its own and stronger together.

---

## 1. A coach who sounds like a coach

You can now choose between twelve named coach personas — each with a distinct voice, a tone, a default outfit, and a system prompt that shapes every reply, reminder, and weekly review.

- Free tier: meet **Alex**, the default coach, plus a small library of free personas.
- Paying tiers progressively unlock more personas, including specialized voices like a strength coach, a clinical psychologist, a zen monk, and a startup mentor.
- Premium tier subscribers can go further and **customize** their coach: outfit pack, voice accent, custom name, gender, relationship style, and a custom system-prompt addition that shapes every interaction.

The avatar art is generated deterministically from a seed, so personas look consistent everywhere they appear: persona picker, plan view, reminders, and the chat surface.

---

## 2. A wizard that builds you a real plan

The new goal wizard runs you through one of three coaching frameworks and produces a plan with milestones, habits, cues, rewards, and a first action — all in under five minutes.

- **GROW** (Whitmore, 1992) — Goal, Reality, Options, Way Forward. The classic conversational coaching loop.
- **WOOP** (Oettingen, 2014) — Wish, Outcome, Obstacle, Plan. Mental-contrasting plus implementation intentions, the strongest evidence base in goal-setting research.
- **Identity** (Clear / Fogg / Wood & Neal) — start from "who you want to become" and build habits that vote for that identity.

The wizard captures rich context, but the validation layer is forgiving: extra fields flow through to the plan generator so you can answer in your own words without fighting the form. Plans are generated by a tier-routed model — `gpt-4o-mini` for free and Starter, `gpt-4o` for Pro, and `o4-mini` reasoning for Premium — so paying tiers get noticeably deeper plans without us pretending it's the same engine for everyone.

---

## 3. Reminders that actually arrive

The reminder engine is new from the ground up. Reminders are scheduled at plan creation, dispatched on a five-minute Vercel cron job, and delivered through three channels: email (via Resend with React Email templates), browser push (via web-push with VAPID), and in-app notifications. A composite-key idempotency check prevents duplicate sends. A watchdog cron picks up any reminder that missed its window.

Five email templates ship in this release:
- Daily habit reminder
- Weekly check-in
- Milestone celebration
- Missed-streak nudge
- Momentum drop alert

Each template is flavored by your selected persona's voice. You control which channels are on and at what times from `/settings/notifications`.

A separate cron job recalculates a per-plan **momentum score** every six hours. Momentum starts at 50, goes up on check-ins and milestone completions, and goes down on missed days — clamped to the 0-100 range so a single bad week never drags you to zero.

## 4. An admin console for coaching plans

For teams using HabitOS at the org level, there is a new admin surface at `/admin/coaching-plans`:

- **List + filter:** by status, tier, framework, persona, date range, free-text search across user email and plan title. Pagination, sorting, and the same filter set drives a CSV export with OWASP formula-injection guards.
- **Detail view:** five tabs covering Overview, Milestones, Habits, Check-ins, and Audit Log.
- **Mutations:** inline edit, pause, resume, stop, soft-delete. Every change is wrapped in a database transaction with an audit log row, so the audit trail is impossible to lose without losing the change too.
- **Analytics library:** `getPlanMetricsSnapshot()` and `getPlanFunnel()` are available to any internal dashboard surface.

See `docs/ADMIN-COACHING-GUIDE.md` for the full enablement walkthrough.

## 5. Four tiers, one Stripe checkout

HabitOS now ships with a four-tier plan ladder and end-to-end Stripe billing.

| Tier | Monthly | Yearly | Headline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | — | One real coached habit, GROW only |
| Starter | $9 | $90 | 3 active goals, all 3 frameworks, basic reminders |
| Pro | $19 | $190 | 10 active goals, smart multi-daily reminders, push, weekly insights, analytics |
| Premium | $39 | $390 | Unlimited goals, deeper weekly review, full coach customization, priority support |

Yearly billing saves about 17 percent on every paid tier. Cancel anytime from the customer portal at `/settings/billing`. Subscription state is the responsibility of a single signed, idempotent webhook — your tier on HabitOS always matches your tier on Stripe within seconds of any change.

Premium-only customization is gated to **outfit pack** and **voice accent**. The other coach customization fields — custom name, gender, relationship style, custom system addition — are available to every paying tier.

## What you should do as an existing user

- **Keep using HabitOS.** Nothing breaks. Your existing habits, check-ins, and goals all still work.
- **Try the wizard.** Open `/goals/new`, pick a goal, and run it through the framework that fits — five minutes will give you a real plan.
- **Pick a persona.** Visit `/coach/personas` and choose the voice that fits how you want to be coached. You can always switch.
- **Open notifications.** `/settings/notifications` is the new home for email, push, and in-app preferences. Push is opt-in and lives entirely in your browser.
- **If you want more goals or smarter reminders, look at Pro or Premium.** No FOMO, no scarcity nudges — just the page at `/pricing` whenever you are ready.

## What is intentionally not in this release

A few things we considered and chose to defer so this release stays focused:

- A native iOS or Android push channel (web push is here today).
- A `User.stripeCustomerId` column for performance — we look up by email each call instead, accepting ~150ms of added latency on checkout / portal endpoints.
- A `cancelAtPeriodEnd` boolean column on `Subscription` — derived heuristically from `canceledAt` + `currentPeriodEnd`.
- Cross-app integrations between HumanOS apps. The hooks are in place; the actual cross-writes are slated for v2.1.
- A dedicated `ProcessedWebhookEvent` table — we use `SiteSettings` as a key/value claim store for now.

These are all tracked in the M6 decisions log (DEC-015 through DEC-018) and will land in subsequent dot releases.
